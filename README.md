# -Historical-Automobile-Sales-Analysis
Exploratory data analysis of historical automobile sales data, with a focus on understanding how economic recessions impact vehicle sales, advertising spend, and consumer behaviour across different vehicle types.
## Dataset
- Features include: `Automobile_Sales`, `Recession`, `Year`, `Month`, `GDP`, `Vehicle_Type`, `Price`, `Advertising_Expenditure`, `Seasonality_Weight`, `unemployment_rate`

## What I Did
- Analyzed trends in automobile sales during recession vs. non-recession periods
- Compared advertising expenditure across economic conditions
- Examined GDP fluctuations during recession and non-recession years
- Investigated the effect of unemployment rate on vehicle sales by type
- Explored seasonality patterns in monthly sales data
- Visualized price vs. sales relationships during recession periods

## Key Visualizations
- Line plot: Advertising expenditure and automobile sales over time (non-recession)
- Bar chart: Average vehicle sales by type during recession vs. non-recession
- Subplots: GDP trends during recession vs. non-recession periods
- Scatter plot: Seasonality weight vs. average monthly sales
- Pie charts: Advertising expenditure split by economic period and vehicle type
- Line plot: Unemployment rate vs. automobile sales by vehicle type during recession

## Tools & Libraries
- Python (Google Colab)
- `pandas` — data manipulation
- `matplotlib` — visualizations
- `seaborn` — statistical plots

## Key Findings
- Automobile sales dropped significantly during recession periods across all vehicle types
- Advertising expenditure was higher during non-recession periods
- Unemployment rate negatively correlated with sales, especially for higher-priced vehicles
- Seasonality had a noticeable effect on monthly sales volume
